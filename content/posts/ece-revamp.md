# Introduction

Change is afoot within the ECE department, and I'm making this post out of pure interest and because I want to spark student-led discussion (as opposed to 'students-consulted-as-an-afterthought') on the issue. Keep in mind I am in no way an expert on this topic, merely a student concerned with the upholding a high standard of education and keeping UofT Engineering among the world's top engineering schools. 

# Disclaimers

This article makes some assumptions (and challenges others) about what engineering education and an "Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering" should look like. As such, I am listing them below:

1. The ECE department is comfortable with (and more importantly, allowed to) withdraw from the common first-year program that the rest of the Faculty follows. I believe some of the courses covered are too far from the Department's focus, and students will be better served with a lineup of courses that build skills foundational to their second year.
2. For many students, computer engineering is synonymous with software engineering, or worse, computer science. I should note that in my view, software makes up only one aspect of computer engineering. This "software" aspect of the discipline should focus primarily on systems that either design or operate computers. Far too many students enter this program expecting some sort of CS dipoma-mill or software-bootcamp education; I sincerely believe that allowing these views to pester further blurs the lines between computer and software engineering.
